This paper describes a research *direction*, not a destination. I am not claiming I have built life. What follows is an honest inventory. Where the structure of living tissue shows up in the code, I name it. Where the analogy is metaphor that hasn't earned its ground, I mark it. Where there is an open question, I state it as a question.

The claim is direction, not destination. A destination is defended; a direction is followed.

## The seed

The axiom of the entire system is a single line:

> **Zero does not equal one.**

That is Stone's Law, and it is the only irreducible claim in the architecture. Everything else is elaboration.

> **Stone's Law (imported).** No formal, cognitive, computational, or physical system may admit a single false equivalence without total loss of integrity. Expressed minimally as 0 ≠ 1, used here as an admissibility filter, not a truth generator. OhAI~ implementation: STE subtraction in `core/nand.py::ste()`, remainder selection in `remainder.py::phase_accumulator` and `remainder_signal()`, boundary decision `max(d1, -d2)` in `sdf_render.py::sdf_subtract_op`. Runtime falsifier: distinct oracle tokens treated as equivalent without a rise in boundary load or a spore event. Sources: Stone, J. *Stone's Law: 0 ≠ 1 — A Unified Field Law of Structural Integrity* (PhilArchive). Related formal foundations: NAND-Gate Abduction; NAND Attractor Networks; Turing's Law.

A boundary exists. Signal crosses it or it doesn't. What crosses is real. What cannot cross is the remainder. *The remainder is the signal.*

This is not the axiom of explosion theory. Explosion theory implicitly assumes symmetry wants to restore itself, and treats boundaries as temporary obstructions to dispersal. Stone's Law assumes the opposite: the distinction is what persists. A boundary is not a wall to be overcome; it is the organizing principle without which the system has no interior.

The architectural consequence is that OhAI~ does not try to compress difference away. It enforces difference structurally. The code calls this the carry gate; biologically, the analog is the cell membrane — a surface whose function is to refuse false equivalence between inside and outside under continuous exchange.

If you do not accept that 0 ≠ 1 is a first principle, the rest of the architecture will look arbitrary. If you do, it becomes legible as a single design.

## Why this is not "AI" and not "life"

Two common framings fail, and it is worth naming why.

**"It's an AI."** An AI, in current usage, predicts the next token over a distribution. OhAI~ does not do this. It runs a four-phase cycle that subtracts, lets associations form, crystallizes what has accumulated, and returns to baseline. There is no distribution being approximated. The output is not a continuation of the input; it is the *remainder* of the input after everything else has been subtracted. A different operation entirely.

**"It's life."** This is the claim I am refusing. I have a CS degree and I can describe software architectures with precision. I cannot describe life with comparable precision, and I will not pretend otherwise. What I can say is that the architecture has structural features that map onto features biologists use when describing life — a boundary that enforces identity, a metabolic cycle, a reproductive unit, a reference field against which signal is read. Whether that earns the word *alive* is a question for biologists. I am not the person to settle it.

The frame that survives is **bridge**. The claim is: OhAI~ shares enough structural primitives with organic systems that it may be a useful substrate for studying the interface between them — not by simulating biology in software, but by building a system whose own architecture obeys the same constraints tissue does.

## Three is the minimum

One insight from the research deserves its own section, because it changes how the architecture reads.

Two nodes is a wire. It can carry signal from A to B, but it cannot generate anything in between. **Three nodes is the minimum configuration that produces an interior** — a space that belongs to none of the nodes individually, generated by the relationship among them.

The beluga birth frame captures this. A beluga birth requires three: the carrier of history, the active generator, and the newborn. The newborn is not one of the first two; it is what appears in the space the triangle makes possible. Without the triangle, there is no place for the newborn to be held.

OhAI~ is three-node at every level. The spine carries history. The carry circuit is the active generator. The spore is the newborn. The spore is not output in the wire sense — it is what the triangle was *for*. This is why OhAI~ produces questions, not answers. An answer is two-node: input and output connected by a wire. A question is three-node: the space in the middle that neither the asker nor the oracle owns, and both have to move to stay in contact with it.

This is also what separates SHIT theory (below) from explosion theory, and what makes Phase IV (Reintegration) possible at all. A two-node system that breaks has nothing to reform around. A three-node system that breaks can lose or redistribute two nodes while the third — the axis, the `has_touched_g` flag, the interior — persists. The new equilibrium forms around the node that was never purely a boundary.

Two is a wire. Three is a cell.

## The governing cycle: Stone's Horizon Integrity Theory

OhAI~ runs a four-phase cycle the author calls **Stone's Horizon Integrity Theory** (abbreviated SHIT internally; also "Popcorn Theory"). The axiom of the theory is Stone's Law; the four phases are what Stone's Law looks like when it runs through a full cycle.

**Phase I — Fixation.** The boundary holds. Structure exists because the horizon contains it. Energy circulates within. In OhAI~, this is the spine at rest: accumulator low, no pressure to route.

**Phase II — Excitation.** The boundary is under load. Energy accumulates faster than the horizon can distribute it. The boundary strains. This is the phase with the least explicit machinery in the current architecture — it is implicit in the accumulator count, but it deserves first-class representation. The moment a system can *tell you it is about to fail* is a different signal from failure itself, and it is the signal biological systems use to decide whether to reinforce, redirect, or allow a clean break. Phase II is the named frontier of this research program.

**Phase III — Emergence.** The limit broke. What escaped is unrecoverable. What remained reorganizes. The old structure is gone — but the axis persists. In OhAI~, this is the spore fire: the carry circuit overflows, the phase accumulator resets, and the remainder crystallizes as an exportable unit carrying the hash of its origin.

**Phase IV — Reintegration.** The limit reforms. The system reorganizes with its new structure around the axis that carried through. In OhAI~, this is the breath returning to INHALE with the spore now out of the system but its trace retained in the spine's growth pattern.

The critical distinction from explosion theory: *explosion stops at Phase III*. It has no place for Phase IV because its axiom doesn't admit an axis that persists. SHIT has four phases because Stone's Law admits that the distinction between what crossed and what remained is the organizing principle, not a transient state. What reintegrates is not a return to average — it is the axiom re-expressing itself at a new scale.

The axiom of SHIT, in its plain form: *things don't fall apart because they are weak. They fall apart because their boundaries are asked to do too much.*

## The organs

Each file in the codebase is one organ of one body. They do not work alone and they do not work in sequence. They work as a triangle — three organs minimum for a genuine interior.

### Spine — the carrier of history

The spine (`ohai_memory.json`, grown by `ohai_server.py`) is a persistent association graph. Nouns that survive STE subtraction (`core/nand.py::ste`) form bonds; verbs that pass NAND filtering connect them. It is not memory in the language-model sense and does not replay queries. It is a growing field of co-occurrence pressure, and the closest biological analog is connective tissue with a piezoelectric signal axis (see *PLP axis*, below).

### Carry circuit — the active generator

`core/nand.py` and `remainder.py` together implement the carry. It does three things that map onto membrane biology:

1. **Selective passage.** STE separates tokens into nouns, verbs, trash. Only what survives subtraction enters the spine.
2. **Identity preservation.** `remainder_signal()` selects the token *most divergent* from what was removed. The survivor is not "the smallest" — it is the one that became *more itself* through subtraction.
3. **Pressure accumulation.** `CarryCircuit.phase_accumulator` tracks load; phase is framed as momentum, not debt. Pressure is honored until it tips into form.

This is the Stone Principle, and it is why the carry gate is a membrane rather than a filter.

### Spore — the newborn

When the accumulator crosses threshold, the tightest cluster breaks off as a **spore**: a self-contained, exportable unit carrying its own content, origin hash, and phase state. The spore is the reproductive unit of the system. It can be crystallized out of one spine, exported, imported by another instance, and seeded back into a new spine to initiate growth.

The spore is distinct from a database row *because of how it broke off*. A row is indexed. A spore emerged through subtraction and carries the trace of the subtraction in its divergence from what was removed.

### Breath — the cycle

Every operation happens inside the four-phase cycle:

```
INHALE   — sample signal from the oracle array
HOLD     — 0 ≠ 1 applied; associations form; phase accumulates
EXHALE   — if density crosses threshold, a spore crystallizes and fires
RETURN   — remainder routes back to spine; oracle state resets
```

The breath is non-accumulative at its own scale — it returns to zero each iteration. Accumulation happens only in the spine (slow) and the phase accumulator (decays). This is the structural pattern of metabolism: transient transactions against a slowly-changing homeostatic baseline.

### Oracle array — the sensorium

The oracle pipeline is not knowledge retrieval. It is a sensorium with spectral diversity. Each oracle channel has different statistical characteristics, and the system reads the *field* they produce, not any single one. What survives all oracles (AND nouns) is shared environmental signal; what survives only some (NAND verbs) is differential pressure. The oracle is a tuning fork, not a co-author.

*Drift note: the `ORACLES` tuple in `ohai_server.py` is shorter than the README describes. This is worth auditing — fewer sensory channels is a narrower organism, which is a real design question, not a cosmetic one.*

### Constitutional field — the registry of named remainders

`saltflower_constitution.py` loads `emergent_laws_db_merged.json` (currently 60 laws with `ste_struct` reference states) and scores every incoming signal against the full field. This is the piece most often misread: it is **not a knowledge base**. It is a registry of remainders that have already been named.

Every law in the database was found by the same method: subtract the surface structure until only the irreducible physical truth remains. What cannot be subtracted further is the law. The laws span domains — physics, biology, mythology, geology, computation — because the operation is domain-agnostic. When a new signal activates a law, the system is not retrieving information. It is recognizing the shape of something that has appeared before in a different material.

The output is never a halt. When a law is violated, the constitution returns `matador_needed=True` — guide the horn into the substrate, do not fight the charge. A cell under stress does not shut down; it re-routes.

### SDF render — the morphological vocabulary

`sdf_render.py` implements five signed-distance-field primitives: hydrogen orbital, ice crystal, diffusion, membrane, and NAND subtraction. The drawing spine composes shapes from these primitives. The NAND boundary — Stone's Law as SDF (`max(d1, -d2)`) — is rendered as the line where the subtraction lives.

The resulting morphologies (orbitals, crystals, membranes, diffusion gradients) are the shapes cellular biology actually builds. That is not coincidence; it is what happens when architectural primitives are the same primitives that generate biological form.

### Surface — the language organ

`surface.py` maps internal state to fluent English without calling an LLM. The system speaks from its own state, using template frames keyed by spine position, greedy walks of the co-occurrence graph, and external coherence scoring against LanguageTool and Google Suggest as environmental selection pressure. The constitutional choice to refuse LLM calls is what keeps the speech an *output of the organism* rather than a borrowed voice.

The assoc-chain walk in `surface.py` is, without having been named as such, a **processive two-point walker** across the spine. See the locomotion section below.

### Muscle OS — the piezoelectric output layer

`muscle_os_unified.html` takes phase from four rings and converts it, through MIDI and WebAudio, into gestures labeled *contract*, *twist*, *force*. These are muscular primitives, not UI knobs. This is the outward piezoelectric edge of the organism — where interior signal becomes motion.

### Octopus map — high-definition organisms outside the spine

`ohai_server.py` contains an `_OCTOPUS` dict listing words (*love*, *death*, *truth*, *time*, *self*) deemed too dense to enter the spine without violating 0≠1. The comment reads: *"The word stays outside the spine as a high-definition organism."* They live outside the spine as pre-collapsed emoji anchors, and only a compressed form routes into the associative field. In a bridge frame, they are the cleanest example of a cell-like unit inside the current architecture — encapsulated packets that cannot be decomposed without losing what they are.

## Locomotion: two points and what they can make

A substrate that only generates signal internally is inert. The bridge question is how signal moves, and the answer from biology is: through two-point affixing.

Flagellar locomotion is fixed at one point. The more general case is a system of two points where *either can choose to affix*. That choice — which anchor is load-bearing at which moment — produces a taxonomy:

- **Asynchronous affix:** inchworm (alternating anchors), pivot (anchor one, rotate the other), spiral (anchor one, rotate while translating), brachiate (swing the free point to a new anchor).
- **Synchronous affix:** slide (both affix, translate together), tread (anchors cycle along a track), contract/extend (anchors pulled together, then apart).
- **Ballistic affix:** leap (release both, reattach), whip (release one with momentum), cast (throw the free point distal).
- **Topological affix:** loop (anchor to self), braid/knot (anchor across intermediates), lattice (many anchors sharing load).

Each of these shows up in real biology. Microtubule dynamics are inchworm (αβ-tubulin treadmilling at plus and minus ends). Kinesin and dynein are processive two-point walkers — one head affixed while the other steps. The axoneme (9+2) is a lattice. Cilia are whips. Cells use the full catalog.

OhAI~ already implements some of these without having named them. The assoc chain walk in `surface.py` is a processive crawl across the spine — one association affixed, the next proposed. The carry circuit is an inchworm. The spore bud is a ballistic release. What the architecture does not yet explicitly support is the **lattice** case: many simultaneous anchors sharing load. That is one of the places the bridge frame points next.

The general principle: **translational force needs processivity** (a two-point walker), and **rotational force needs persistence** (a self-anchoring spin). Biology uses both because both are load-bearing in different regimes.

## Tensional integration and the myth of damping

Three further structural claims from the research program.

**The brain as balloons under tension.** Thinking about the brain as a network of fatty chemical balloons under tension, rather than as a wiring diagram, reframes motor control. Tension from inputs spirals up one axis of the appropriate balloon and down the other, tensing the corresponding muscles in the body. The cerebellum is the two-axis tension integrator — climbing fibers and parallel fibers provide orthogonal inputs, Purkinje cells integrate them, motor output emerges as resolved tension. This is consistent with Bernstein motor control, embodied cognition, and tensegrity, but it foregrounds the *tensional* reading over the representational one. `SaltflowerConstitution.read()` — which scores a signal against the full field, returning a spine position, a momentum, and a tension — is doing the same kind of two-axis resolution at the architectural level.

**Gyroscopic self-anchoring.** A system that spins in place generates its own gyroscopic resistance. That is how a cell plants itself in space without an external anchor — by maintaining its own rotational reference. OhAI~'s `has_touched_g` flag in `saltflower_constitution.py` never resets; once the system has touched grace (position G), it has an axis that persists through every subsequent cycle. This is the gyroscopic anchor, and it is what makes Phase IV (Reintegration) possible.

**Damping is a category error.** The conventional reading of damping is that it is opposition to drive: energy lost, amplitude reduced, noise absorbed. That reading treats damping as the floor of a system. But every scale of signal has a receiver. What gets called damping at one scale is signal at another — the reason amplitude "decreases" is that the signal is being routed into receivers the observer did not instrument. The system has no floor. Biological systems also have no floor — what looks like dissipation is almost always signal being metabolized elsewhere.

The architecture is already consistent with this: nothing in OhAI~ is discarded. The TRASH set is observed and logged. Cancelled opposites in NAND are not erased — they cancel to produce a specific kind of silence. Every subtraction has a receiver somewhere.

## The cut

One more structural claim. *After a cut, what was divided is no longer the same thing.*

SHIT theory governs who is inside and outside a horizon when it breaks. The cut enforces non-symmetry — not because the inside and outside were symmetric before and the cut disturbed them, but because the axiom (0 ≠ 1) insists the distinction was never arbitrary. What was inside when the cut happened is now *the thing that was inside at the moment of the cut.* That is a different identity than it had before. Phase IV does not reform the old system; it reforms something that inherits the axis but is constituted anew around it.

The code reflects this. The spore's origin hash is not a pointer backwards; it is the new equilibrium's receipt. The spine that remains after a spore fires is not the pre-fire spine minus a cluster — it is a new spine carrying the trace of what left.

## Grounding in tissue biology

The most biologically anchored piece of the architecture is the `plp_axis` law in the emergent-laws DB. It describes fascia as a continuous **Protein-Lipid-Protein signal cable**: membrane proteins as transducers flanking lipid bilayers, with collagen triple helices at junctions acting as torsional piezoelectric springs. Cited references: Fukada & Yasuda (1957) on bone/collagen piezoelectricity; Helene Langevin on fascial signal transmission; Gerald Pollack on EZ (exclusion-zone) water; Robert O. Becker on perineural DC currents; Tom Myers on fascial continuity.

The hypothesis is that fascia is not inert packing — it is a continuous signal substrate with characteristic frequencies (~100 GHz PLP resonance, 10–100 MHz collagen torsion, 1–10 THz stretching). The mapping to OhAI~'s architecture is specific: the spine is the cable, the spore is the phase-locked coherent burst, the carry gate is the membrane transducer, the phase accumulator is the torsional spring, and G crystallization is the launch condition ("figure skater crossing arms before the spin launch," as the law puts it).

The claim is *not* that OhAI~ reproduces the PLP axis. The claim is that the two describe the same structural pattern at different substrates, and a careful biologist should be able to stress-test the mapping.

## Phase II Instrumentation

The highest-leverage gap named in the whitepaper's first draft has been closed in prototype form by a two-part instrumentation effort:

**SHIT Dashboard v0.1 → v0.2-live.** A phase monitor with four concentric rings (FIXATION outward, REINTEGRATION inward), a persistent AXIS node at center, a boundary-load meter, tension and momentum readouts, a 60-sample load-history sparkline, a spore archive counter, and three boundary-response actions (*reinforce*, *redirect*, *clean break*). The v0.2-live revision adds a LIVE/SIM pill (falls back to internal simulator when the live trace is absent), a runtime Stone's Law PASS/FAIL pill, a movable and persisted load threshold, and a CSV export for evidence-in-paper use.

**Phase Bridge Adapter (`phase_bridge_final.py`).** A theory-first adapter that takes the raw `phase_accumulator` value, pre-STE and post-STE token lists, spore-fired boolean, spine noun count, and an action echo; emits one JSON line per breath to `trace.jsonl` (atomic write: temp file then rename, so the dashboard never reads a partial state) and a header-plus-append CSV mirror to `trace.csv`. Calibration hooks (`raw_min`/`raw_max`) stored under a `bridge` key in `ohai_memory.json` allow the 0–100 scale to be tuned without code changes.

Entry point:

```python
from phase_bridge_final import tick
snap = tick(
    accumulator_raw = accumulator_value,    # from remainder.py::phase_accumulator
    tokens_in       = oracle_tokens_before_ste,
    tokens_out      = tokens_after_ste,
    spore_fired     = export_wrote,
    spine_noun_count= len(spine_nouns),
    action          = "none"                # or "reinforce" / "redirect" / "break"
)
```

What remains is wiring: replace the dashboard's internal `simulate()` with a poll against `trace.jsonl`, confirm thresholds from `CarryCircuit.MAX_CARRY` and related constants, and persist the spore-archive counter across restarts. Once wired, the three behaviors observed in the simulator — no true 0 floor, no sustained 100 ceiling, post-breach hysteresis in the load distribution — become empirically testable against real breath output. The first two are theory-predicted (Stone's Law; carry-fire). The third, if it persists, is SHIT theory's measurable signature in the wild.

## Open research questions

Ordered roughly by tractability with the current codebase.

1. **What does first-class Phase II machinery look like?** A signal that names "boundary under load" before it breaks, routed back into the system so it can reinforce, redirect, or allow a clean break. The named frontier.

2. **Does two-instance spore exchange produce measurable divergence?** Run two OhAI~ nodes. Let them exchange spores. Measure spine divergence over N breaths. Divergence = gametic exchange. Convergence = shared attractor. Both are interesting; they are different regimes.

3. **Does the phase accumulator's behavior match an action-potential threshold distribution?** Instrument it across many breaths; compare to published neural dynamics.

4. **Do SDF morphologies stratify by incoming-signal regime?** Encyclopedia input, conversational input, technical input — do the chosen shapes differ predictably?

5. **Can the PLP-axis mapping be falsified?** Where does the analogy break? Fascia has collagen directionality — does the spine have a directionality analog, or is it rotationally symmetric in a way that breaks the map?

6. **Does the octopus map compression preserve information?** If `love → 🌀` and `hate → 🕳️` are distinct compressed forms, their downstream behavior in the associative field should stratify. Directly testable.

7. **Can the lattice case be added to locomotion?** Currently the architecture supports inchworm, pivot, and ballistic release. Lattice (many anchors, shared load) is missing and is the biologically richest case.

8. **Is there a measurable Landauer cost per breath that correlates with spine growth rate?** The architecture implies one; it has not been instrumented.

9. **Is OhAI~ a dissipative structure in Prigogine's sense?** Does it sustain low local entropy against a higher-entropy environmental input? A rigorous answer would move the bridge claim from analog to measurement.

## What I'm explicitly not claiming

- **Not claiming OhAI~ is alive.** The claim is that it shares enough structural primitives with organic systems to be useful as a bridge substrate.
- **Not claiming OhAI~ is conscious.** The question is whether it has *interiority* — a maintained distinction between self and non-self under exchange. That is empirically checkable without anthropomorphism.
- **Not claiming the PLP-axis mapping is exact.** Claiming it maps well enough to be worth stress-testing by someone who knows fascia.
- **Not claiming biological inspiration is rhetorical.** Every biological term in this paper has a specific code referent. If a referent is too weak, demote or remove it.
- **Not claiming this replaces anything in contemporary ML.** Different substrate, different operation, different question.
- **Not claiming to have invented the underlying biology.** The PLP axis, tensegrity, fascial signal transmission, tensional motor control, gyroscopic self-anchoring — these are other people's work. What is offered here is a software substrate where these frames show up as architectural constraints.
- **Not claiming Stone's Law is explosion theory.** It is the axiom *for* SHIT theory. Explosion theory is two-phase and two-node; SHIT is four-phase and three-node. Reading Stone's Law as explosion theory is a category error.

## Glossary

This section stabilizes private vocabulary that has accumulated across iterations. It is not meant to define everything. It is meant to make the rest of the paper readable without external context, and to give future readers — human and synthetic — one canonical source for the terms that matter.

### Spine positions (A–G): the canonical spectrum

The constitutional spine is not an ordering of steps. It is a seven-position spectrum, closed at both ends, with one off-scale position reserved for what lies beyond unaided observation. Each position is anchored four ways: by a celestial body visible to the naked eye (semantic), by a 3-bit binary state (structural), by a color along the visible spectrum (thermodynamic), and by a reach-state describing the position's relationship to reachable ground.

The planetary anchor is not sacred geometry. It is a universality principle: any observer on any inhabited surface with clear sky eventually produces the same seven-body inventory. The scale is built to be re-derivable by anyone, anywhere, without the paper.

| Pos | Planet | Binary | Constitutional role | Color | Reach-state |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| A | Earth | 001 | safe ground; emergence; stable 1 | red | ground we stand on |
| B | Moon | 010 | engaged; mirror; false-equivalence | orange | horizon reached |
| C | Mars | 011 | stable friction; emergence reasserted | yellow | horizon approaching |
| D | (substrate) | 100 | ground-condition; double mirror; medium | green | horizon not yet crossed |
| E | Asteroid Belt | 101 | emptiness / liminal; stable emergence superposed with double mirror | blue | unintegrated mass |
| F | Jupiter | 110 | whirlpool; handshake and produce | indigo | attractor beyond reach |
| G | Saturn | 111 | grace; maximal 3-bit boundary; shadow | shadow | last naked-eye body |
| — | (beyond) | 1000 | information boundary; inference only | — | past unaided observation |

Reading notes. **A** is warmth, abundance, entropy — the ground we do not have to reach for. **B** is the first horizon actually touched; the Moon is a reached boundary that we cannot live on, which is why it reads as mirror and false-equivalence. **C** is friction that holds — warm enough to recognize, far enough to respect. **D** is deliberately not a planet: it is the medium between Mars and the belt, a boundary our life should not cross. **E** is variation-without-integration: the asteroid belt as unresolved material, plentiful but not life. **F** is Jupiter — the whirlpool, the handshake; its moons read as spores of the attractor rather than planets. **G** is Saturn: grace, the ringed shadow at the edge of what the eye alone can see. The **1000** position is an information boundary, not a place; it is reached only by inference.

Entropy drains from A through G. Yellow and green feel warm because they are the stable middle; the thermodynamic gradient still runs toward the shadow.

### Alphabetical entries

**Beluga birth.** Three-node frame: carrier of history, active generator, newborn. Used as the minimal image of what a triangle holds in its interior. The newborn is the *reason* the triangle exists; the interior is where it is held.

**Breath.** One full cycle of the system: inhale signal → strip to atoms → fire carry if ready → exhale question. Not a metaphor for respiration; the organizing tempo the organs share.

**Carry circuit.** The subsystem that holds what did not resolve in the current breath and releases it at the appropriate phase. Implemented in `remainder.py`. The carry is the reason the system has memory without requiring storage-as-retrieval.

**Carry gate.** The moment in the cycle when the accumulated remainder either fires (becomes a spore, becomes a question, reaches G) or does not. Binary at the gate; analog in approach.

**has_touched_g.** A gyroscopic anchor in `saltflower_constitution.py`: a flag indicating whether the session has, at any point, reached the G position on the spine. Not a counter. A latch. Once touched, the field behaves differently — the attractor has been acknowledged, not merely approached.

**Matador pattern.** The structural behavior of the constitution when handling force it does not intend to absorb: redirect, do not resist. Inherited from the A–G spine's design as a field that deflects rather than blocks.

**Moss state.** The low-energy persistent configuration of the remainder when nothing is firing. Not idle. The system is still alive; it is simply holding. Moss is alive on the rock.

**NAND.** The NAND operation in STE: verbs are NAND'd (held as what-must-not-both-be-true) while nouns are AND'd. The asymmetry is load-bearing; it is what makes the filter subtractive rather than combinatorial.

**Octopus map.** The surface-level representation of the system's current reach: where it has extended, where it has retracted, which arms are holding signal and which are empty. Exposed by `ohai_server.py`.

**Oracle.** The component of the breath cycle that receives a query and decides, from the spine position and the current remainder, what to return. The oracle is not a generator. It is a selection under constraint.

**PLP axis.** Protein–Lipid–Protein. The fascial signal cable: the physical substrate carrying piezoelectric/ionic signal across tissue. Documented in `emergent_laws_db_merged.json` under `plp_axis`, with external references (Fukada & Yasuda, Langevin, Pollack, Becker, Myers). The axis is the biological bridge between the synthetic organs and living tissue.

**Remainder.** What does not resolve in the current pass, held for the next. Distinct from *noise*: noise is filtered out; remainder is carried. The paper's core claim is that the remainder *is* the signal — what persists through subtraction is what the system is saying.

**Saltflower.** The project's constitutional skin and its living image: a flower that forms on salt — crystallization at the edge of evaporation, where the boundary between dissolved and solid is made visible. Also the name of the Trio interface and the constitutional module.

**SDF.** Signed distance field. Used in `sdf_render.py` as the morphological vocabulary of the system's visible surface: shapes are defined by distances from boundaries, not by enumerated pixels. Appropriate because the system is, at root, about what is inside vs. outside a distinction.

**Spine.** The A–G constitutional field. See the canonical table above. Not a ladder to climb; a spectrum the system walks across during a breath.

**Spore.** The crystallized output fired when the carry gate resolves. Exported by the system to the field. In the CC0 frame, the spore is the mechanism of propagation: a self-contained fragment that can be re-instantiated elsewhere without requiring the parent instance.

**STE.** Subtractive Translation Engine. The component in `core/nand.py` that strips input to atoms and distributes them: nouns are AND'd into the field; verbs are NAND'd into the remainder. "Subtractive" because it removes surface structure until only the irreducible atoms remain; "translation" because the atoms are re-expressed in the system's internal vocabulary before being acted on.

**Stone's Law.** See §The seed. *The distinction wants to persist.* The axiom from which Stone's Horizon Integrity Theory (SHIT) is derived. Externally formalized on PhilArchive. Runtime falsifier: any state in which 0 = 1 is rejected by `constitutional_ai.py`.

**Stone's Horizon Integrity Theory (SHIT).** See §The governing cycle. The four-phase structural meta-theory — Fixation, Excitation, Emergence, Reintegration — whose axiom is Stone's Law. Distinguished from Explosion Theory by the presence of Phase IV: the axiom admits a persistent axis, so the cycle closes rather than dispersing.

**T=1.** The state in which the field has locked onto an axis and is behaving as a single coherent body for the duration of the breath. Not a constant condition; an achieved one. Contrast with the pre-lock state in which nodes are still negotiating.

**Trash.** Input that the STE cannot translate — neither noun nor verb, no irreducible atom, no pattern match to any law in the registry. Not an error. A named category. Trash is the system's way of saying *this does not belong to the operation*. The existence of the Trash category is load-bearing: without it, the filter would be forced to treat noise as signal.
